Custom Die-Cut Aluminum Blanks: Everything You Need to Know

April 23, 2026 · 7 min read

Standard rectangles and circles are great for many products. But if you want your sublimation business to stand out — pet tags shaped like bones, keychains with your logo's exact outline, or commemorative badges with unique silhouettes — you need custom die-cut blanks.

Here's what the custom die-cut process looks like from start to finish, and what you need to know before getting started.

What Does "Die-Cut" Mean?

Die-cutting is a manufacturing process where a specialized tool (called a die or mold) stamps raw material into a specific shape. Think of it like a cookie cutter, but industrial-strength and precision-engineered to tolerances of ±0.1mm.

For aluminum sublimation blanks, the process works like this: aluminum coils are coated with the sublimation layer, then fed into a stamping press where the die cuts each blank to your exact shape. The result is a precisely shaped, consistently sized blank ready for heat press printing.

File Formats We Accept

To create your custom die, we need a vector file of your shape. Acceptable formats include:

  • DXF — AutoCAD exchange format (most common)
  • AI — Adobe Illustrator
  • CDR — CorelDRAW
  • EPS — Encapsulated PostScript
  • PDF — Vector PDF (not raster/image PDF)

The key requirement is that the file must be a true vector — not a bitmap or raster image. Vector files define shapes mathematically, so they scale without losing quality and allow our engineers to extract the exact cutting path.

File Checklist

- Outline is a single closed path (no gaps or overlaps)
- Minimum internal corner radius: 1mm (sharp 90° corners are harder to cut cleanly)
- Maximum overall size: typically within 200mm on any dimension
- Include your desired dimensions in the file or in a reference document

The Production Process

Step 1: Design Review

Send us your vector file and specifications (size, thickness, quantity). Our engineering team reviews the design for manufacturability — checking for any features that might cause issues during stamping (sharp internal corners, very narrow bridges, etc.). We'll suggest minor adjustments if needed.

Step 2: Die Manufacturing

Once the design is confirmed, we create the stamping die. This involves precision CNC machining and wire EDM (electrical discharge machining) to cut the die steel to your exact shape. This step typically takes 7–14 business days.

Step 3: Sample Approval

We stamp a small batch of samples using the new die and send them to you. You test the samples on your heat press to verify size, shape, and coating quality. Any adjustments to the die (if needed) are made at this stage.

Step 4: Mass Production

After sample approval, we move to full production. Aluminum coils are cut to size, coated with the sublimation layer, and fed through the stamping press. Every batch undergoes quality inspection for dimensions, coating integrity, and surface finish.

Step 5: Packing and Shipping

Finished blanks are packed per your requirements — bulk poly bags, individual sleeves, or custom packaging. We ship worldwide with full tracking.
