Ordering & Pricing

What is the MOQ for aluminum sublimation blanks?

For standard shapes (CR80, oversized, round), the MOQ is 500 pieces. For testing purposes, we accept orders as low as 10 pieces. Custom die-cut shapes require a minimum order of 50,000 pieces plus mold cost starting from ¥3,000.

Send us your requirements (size, thickness, quantity, coating type) via email at 370048439@qq.com, WhatsApp, or our contact form. We reply with a detailed quote within 24 hours, including pricing, lead time, and shipping options.

Do prices include shipping?

+
Our quoted prices are typically FOB (Free on Board) — they cover production and delivery to the port. Shipping freight is quoted separately based on your preferred method (sea or air) and destination. We work with reliable freight forwarders to get you competitive shipping rates.

Products & Specifications

What thickness options are available?

+
We offer aluminum blanks in 0.22mm, 0.30mm, 0.40mm, 0.50mm, and 0.55mm thickness. The most popular thickness is 0.55mm for business cards and badges. Thinner options (0.22-0.30mm) are ideal for flexible applications like bookmarks and labels.

What standard sizes do you offer?

+
Standard sizes include: CR80 cards (86×54mm), oversized cards (115×54mm), 100×40mm cards, and round blanks (70mm diameter). We can also produce custom sizes for orders of 500+ pieces.

What is the difference between 0.55mm and 0.30mm thickness?

+
0.55mm is the standard CR80 credit card thickness — rigid and durable, ideal for business cards, membership cards, and badges.

0.30mm is flexible and thinner, suitable for labels, bookmarks, and lightweight applications where some bendability is desired.

Can you produce custom die-cut shapes?

+
Yes! We manufacture custom shapes from your vector files (DXF, AI, CDR, EPS, PDF). Mold cost starts from ¥3,000 (approximately $420 USD). MOQ for custom shapes is 50,000 pieces. We handle prototyping, mold making, and mass production in-house.
Quality & Coating

How do you ensure coating quality?

+
Every production batch undergoes adhesion testing. We check for peeling resistance, color transfer quality, and surface uniformity. Our sublimation coating is specifically formulated for heat press applications — no ghosting, no peeling, vibrant color reproduction.

Are there burrs on the edges of stamped blanks?

+
The front face of all blanks is completely burr-free. On the back side, minimal burrs may be present due to the stamping manufacturing process. We polish our dies regularly after every batch to keep burrs to an absolute minimum.

What heat press settings do you recommend?

+
While settings vary by press model, a general starting point: 180-200°C (356-392°F) for 60-90 seconds with medium pressure. We always recommend testing on sample blanks first to dial in the perfect settings for your specific equipment.
